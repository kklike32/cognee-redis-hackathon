## TL;DR (10 second read)

Deel is the category leader. They win on platform breadth, support speed, and brand. We win on compliance depth, country coverage in emerging markets, and dedicated CSM relationships. Lead with trap questions on classification risk and EMEA edge cases. Avoid head to head feature wars at the top of funnel.

---

## 1. Strengths to acknowledge (do not deny)

Pulled from `/wiki/deel.md` Section 5. Frame for the AE:

1. **Platform breadth.** "Deel is the broadest bundle in this category. If you need EOR + HRIS + IT + Equity in one contract, they have that today and we do not."
2. **24/7 multi channel support.** "Their 1 minute chat response is real. If 24/7 live chat is non negotiable, that's their pick."
3. **Category brand and investor default.** "Deel is the first name most investor portfolios reach for. That has weight, especially when your board has portcos on Deel."

## 2. Weaknesses to attack

Pulled from `/wiki/deel.md` Section 6. These are the levers.

1. **Classification depth in regulated EU jurisdictions.** Germany, France, Netherlands. "Have they walked you through which entity structure applies to senior IC roles in Germany?"
2. **Transfer fees and pricing opacity.** Anchor on the G2 evidence: undisclosed transfer fees of ~$4K per year for a 200 person customer.
3. **CSM turnover at the 6 month mark.** Pair with our named CSM + named compliance partner story.

## 3. When They Say / You Say (objection responses)

**"Deel is the leader, why pick anyone else?"**
*You say:* "Leadership in this category is segment dependent. Deel leads on platform breadth. If your priority is compliance depth in regulated jurisdictions, the leader for that profile is us. What's the top compliance risk in your hiring plan over the next 12 months?"

**"Deel covers more countries."**
*You say:* "Deel covers 110 for EOR. We cover 180. Check the country list for your specific markets. We are particularly strong in [emerging market] where Deel relies on partner entities rather than owned entities."

**"Deel's 24/7 support is better than your ticket model."**
*You say:* "Ticket first is intentional. Every Oyster customer has a named CSM and a named compliance partner. We sacrifice 3am chat for a relationship that knows your context. Talk to any of our customers about how that lands when an audit hits."

**"Deel is cheaper."**
*You say:* "Deel's Standard tier is cheaper. Their Plus tier is comparable to us. The total cost question is what you're paying for risk you carry. Did you see the recent classification audit story we shared?"

## 4. Trap setting discovery questions

1. "When you hire a Senior Engineer in Germany on Deel's standard contract, who carries the reclassification risk if local labor courts rule it's an employment relationship?"
2. "Deel's support tickets are 1 minute response on chat. What's the average time to talk to someone who actually knows the labor law of [target country]?"
3. "Deel shipped Mobility in March. Have they implemented visa processes for the specific country corridors you need, or is it on the roadmap?"

## 5. Case studies mapped to ICP and customer need

| ICP stage | Customer need | Use this case |

Series B fintech, 80 employees, US + UK + Germany. Initially chose Deel for speed. 9 months later, German contractor classification failed audit. Three contractors reclassified as employees with back tax exposure ~$220K. Oyster onboarded in 6 weeks with full classification review and ongoing compliance partner. (Source: Internal CSM notes, 2026-03)

**Case B — EU consolidation:**
HR tech Series B, 200 employees. Consolidated 4 EU contractor relationships and 2 EOR contracts onto Oyster for unified compliance reporting. Reduced legal review burden ~60% per quarter. (Source: Internal CSM notes, 2026-02)

**Case C — Seed first UK hire:**
B2B SaaS Seed, 22 employees. First UK hire. Founder had been burned by an EOR audit at a prior company so chose compliance focused EOR. White glove onboarding in 48 hours. (Source: Internal CSM notes, 2026-04)

**Case D — Won back from Deel:**
Same customer as Case A. Originally lost to Deel, won back 9 months later when classification failure surfaced. Reference customer now.

**Case E — Insurtech multi market:**
Insurtech Series B, 140 employees, US + UK + Netherlands + Germany. Chose Oyster over Deel because compliance officer required country specific legal counsel relationship as condition of vendor selection. (Source: Internal CSM notes, 2026-Q1)

## 6. We Win When / We Lose When (honest positioning)

**We win when:**
- Prospect has a compliance failure scar in their history
- Hiring in regulated EU/UK markets (Germany, Netherlands, France especially)
- CFO is in the deal (risk lens beats feature lens)
- Prospect values relationships over self serve
- Insurtech, healthtech, or fintech buyer with compliance officer
- B Corp or mission aligned brand resonates

**We lose when:**
- Prospect is under 50 employees and treats EOR as commodity
- Prospect needs bundled HRIS + IT + Equity (Deel has more bundle breadth)
- Prospect requires 24/7 multi channel live support
- Deal escalates to CPO/CFO who already standardized on Deel for portfolio companies
- Pricing is the only filter at top of funnel

## 7. Trending intelligence (Oyster competitive lens)

This section reads `/wiki/deel.md` Section 7 (growth) and Section 8 (recent activity) and interprets through Oyster's lens.

### Deel's emerging strengths we should watch

- **March 2026 Mobility launch.** Landing in mid market. Reduces vendor count for buyers, pulling deals from Topia/Envoy. **Our response:** acknowledge, then anchor on country specific compliance depth as the offsetting differentiator.
- **Slack/Teams support integration.** Showing up in won deals as "delightful." Lowers switching cost to Deel. **Our response:** emphasize relationship continuity over convenience.

### Deel's emerging weaknesses we should attack

- **Crypto payout option** generating G2 complaints from CFOs. **Our response:** new CFO call objection. "If your CFO does not want crypto exposure on the balance sheet, that's one less thing to disable on day one with us."
- **Transfer fee surprises** appearing in won deal post mortems. **Our response:** add discovery question. "Did Deel disclose all transfer fees up front? Our pricing is one number, no later surprises."
- **CSM turnover at 6 months** flagged in G2 reviews. **Our response:** lead with our named CSM model plus the compliance partner relationship.

### Where Deel is winning lately (against us)

- Late stage Series B and C deals in US tech (bundled product story compounds)
- AI engineering heavy startups (Deel bundled into many YC standard stacks)
- Investor portfolio standardization plays (one Deel contract per VC fund)

### Where we are winning lately (against Deel)

- German and Netherlands contractor classification audits (compliance reputation compounding)
- Customers returning post Deel audit failure (3 such logos in Q1 2026)
- HR tech and insurtech buyers (compliance native positioning resonates)
- Deals where the CFO has a prior audit scar

- Always pull the Case A audit story when classification comes up.
- If the prospect has a CFO with audit experience, change the meeting to a risk conversation, not a feature comparison.
